What is PAG? Pima Association of Governments is a metropolitan planning organization for the greater Tucson region and an association of local, state and tribal governments that works to build consensus among its members and the public on regional planning for transportation, water quality, air quality and economic vitality. To address regional issues through cooperative efforts and pooled resources, and to provide accurate, relevant data that leads to effective regional planning decisions What is the RTA? PAG serves as the for water quality, air quality and solid waste RTA manages the Sun Shuttle service for the region. The Regional Transportation Authority is a political subdivision managed by PAG through a memorandum of understanding. It is the fiscal manager of a $2.1 billion, 20-year regional transportation plan approved by voters in 2006 along with a half-cent excise tax to fund the plan. Who governs PAG and the RTA? PAG and the RTA are governed by a nine-member Regional Council/RTA Board. These governing bodies include the chief elected official or appointed official from each member jurisdiction and the governor-appointed Pima County representative from the Arizona State Transportation Board.
